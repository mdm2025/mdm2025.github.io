The 26th IEEE International Conference on Mobile Data Management

June 2nd - June 5th

Irvine, USA

IEEE MDM 2025: Call for Workshop Proposals

The 26th edition of the IEEE International Conference on Mobile Data Management will be held in Irvine, California, US, from June 2 to June 5, 2025. This single-track international conference aims to foster exchanges of innovative and significant research results in mobile data management. Part of the activities in MDM are centered around workshops, which provide a stimulating environment for exchanging results, findings, and perspectives focused on a particular theme.

In this spirit, MDM 2025 invites submissions for workshop proposals on all topics covered by the conference and other related innovative topics. The workshops are expected to be held on June 2 2025, the day before the main conference. Workshops may be half-day or full-day events.

The logistics of the workshops will be handled by the MDM 2025 organization. Specifically, MDM 2025 will take care of facilities, catering, and the registration process pertinent to all hosted workshops.

The organizers of accepted workshops are required to announce the workshop and call for papers, solicit submissions, invite and coordinate with PC members to ensure that controversial submissions are resolved, and decide upon the final workshop program that must be published at least two weeks before the early registration deadline. The submission deadline for the workshops must be set 1-2 weeks after the notification date of the main conference. The organizers of each workshop must collaborate with the Proceedings Chairs for the purpose of ensuring timely completion of the camera-ready copies before April 10 April 25, 2025, when official conference proceedings are due. A workshop paper is allowed up to 6 proceedings pages.

Publication of the call-for-papers and participation, as well as eventual workshop reports in various outlets, are encouraged.

Proposal evaluation criteria include the following: relevance to MDM, scientific quality, perceived interest and past performance (e.g., previous submission counts and/or workshop participants), and overlap with other proposals.

Important dates:

- the deadline for receiving proposals to be co-located with MDM 2025 is: January 3 January 17, 2025.
- Final notification of acceptance will occur by: January 20, 2025.

Submission Guidelines

Proposals need to include the following:
• Title and Acronym for the workshop.
• Concise description of the workshop scope/theme along with a (tentative) list of topics.
• Preliminary list of (potential) PC members.
• Important milestones for the workshop (e.g., announcements, submission).
• Proposed duration (half-day or full-day workshop).
• Names and contact info and Bio-sketches of the organizers.
• If the organizers have had such/similar workshops held in the past, a brief history/description.
• Past performance (if applicable), e.g., previous submission counts and workshop participants.

Please submit the workshop proposals as a PDF attachment to the workshop program co-chairs (please CC all of them).

A verification response will be returned for each submission.

The review process will be single-blind (i.e., the reviewers are not identified).

Workshop co-chairs:

• Goce Trajcevski (Iowa State University, USA)
• Kyungbaek Kim (Chonnam National University, Korea)
