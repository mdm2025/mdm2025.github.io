The 26th IEEE International Conference on Mobile Data Management

June 2nd - June 5th

Irvine, USA

IEEE MDM 2025: Call for Demo and Poster Track Papers

The IEEE MDM 2025 demo and poster track solicits the submission of high-quality research, systems and platform, product demonstrations (for demos), and exhibits from both academia and industry. We strongly encourage demos based on novel applications in the area of mobile data management that showcase cutting-edge and innovative research and development outcomes. For posters we solicit novel, early-stage research and experimental results (that may down the road lead to a full research paper).

Both posters and demos are an important part of MDM 2025. They provide researchers and research practitioners with an exciting and highly interactive way to demonstrate their research and development outcomes.

Topics of Interest include, but not limited to:

- Machine Learning/AI for Mobile Data
- Human Mobility Modelling
- Synthetic Mobility Data generation
- Human Mobility Prediction
- Novel Data Science Applications on Mobile Data
- Data Management in Mobile Cloud and Edge Computing
- Impact of AI on Human Mobility
- Data Management for Big and Small (Sensor Data)
- Internet of Things (IoT) and Mobile Smartphone-based and Wearable Sensor Systems
- Data Management for Industrial Internet of Things
- Data Management for Mobile Augmented, Virtual and Mixed Reality Systems
- Data Management for Connected Cars, Autonomous Vehicles, Intelligent Transportation Systems, and Smart Spaces
- Data Management in Mobile Health and Epidemics
- Mobile Crowd-Sourcing and Crowd-Sensing
- Behavioral/Activity Sensing and Analytics
- Mobile Location-Based Positioning Systems, Social Networks, Trajectory Analysis, and Tracking Technologies
- Mobile Recommendation Systems (Routing, Personalized Routing, Eco-Routing, Routing for Electrical Vehicles)
- Context-aware Computing for Intelligent Mobile Services
- Middleware and Tools for Mobile and Pervasive Computing
- Data Stream Processing in Mobile/Sensor Network
- Indexing, Optimization and Query Processing for Moving Objects/Users
- Security and Privacy in Mobile Systems
- Transportation-As-A-Service, Mobility-As-A-Service, Data-As-A-Service
- Innovative Applications and real-world deployments driven by Mobile Data

Important dates:

- Submission deadline: March 20, 2025.
- Notification date: April 3 April 17, 2025.
- Camera ready deadline: April 10> April 25, 2025.

Submission Guidelines

Demonstration and poster papers will be evaluated by a minimum 2 of program committee members to assess the novelty of the research/systems/solution as well as how well the audience can be engaged during the conference by a demo and/or poster. It is therefore in the author’s best interest to discuss in the paper with sufficient details, and in the case of a demonstrations how it will be conducted and what aspects thereof will be highlighted in the demo. For example, if your demonstration is web-based, you can include a link to your demonstration which will provide a much higher chance of interactivity with the audience that will help the reviewers during assessments. Both demo and poster submissions should follow these guidelines:

• Page limit: 2-4 pages, including all figures, tables, and references.
• For demos, please include in the paper under a section “Demonstration” - how demonstrations will be conducted and what aspects thereof will be highlighted in the demo.
• Please use the following URL for paper submissions: link.
• All submissions need to follow IEEE Computer Society Proceedings Manuscript Formatting Guidelines. See templates here: https://www.ieee.org/conferences/publishing/templates.html.

Note that a paper exceeding the page limit may be rejected without review. If there are any appendices, they are counted within the page limit. For demos, papers failing to highlight the demonstration aspect may not be considered for the Demo Track and may be rejected.
